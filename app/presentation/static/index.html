How We Measure Impact

Weave Impact ranks contributors by their Knowledge Sharer score — a measure of how much a contributor elevates the quality of other people's work through thoughtful code review. Rather than rewarding raw commit volume or line counts, this model recognises the reviewers who catch real issues, spread knowledge across different parts of the codebase, and help the whole team ship better software.

🔍 Meaningful Comments — × 1.5 each

A comment counts as meaningful when it contains more than 30 characters of actual text and is not a stock approval phrase. The goal is to capture comments that carry substance: a bug spotted, an edge case raised, an alternative design proposed, or a clear explanation of why something needs to change.

Excluded phrases: LGTM +1 looks good approved nice ok and similar one-liners
🗺️ Cross-Subsystem Reviews — × 5.0 each

When a reviewer leaves comments on files that span two or more top-level directories in a single PR, it signals broad architectural awareness — they can reason across the frontend, backend, infra, or data layers at once. This is rare and highly valuable, so it carries the largest weight in the model.

Example: commenting on both frontend/ and posthog/ files in the same PR counts as one cross-subsystem review
